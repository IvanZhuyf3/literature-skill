# A metabolite of daidzein, 6,7,4- -trihydroxyisoflavone, suppresses adipogenesis in 3T3-L1 preadipocytes via ATP-competitive inhibition of PI3K

Scope: Daidzein is one of the major soy isoflavones. Following ingestion, daidzein is readily metabolized in the liver and converted into hydroxylated metabolites. One such metabolite is 6,7,4 -trihydroxyisoflavone (6,7,4 -THIF), which has been the focus of recent studies due to its various health benefits, however, its anti-adipogenic activity has not been investigated. Our objective was to determine the effects of 6,7,4 -THIF on adipogenesis in 3T3-L1 preadipocytes and elucidate the mechanisms of action involved.

Methods and results: Adipogenesis was stimulated in 3T3-L1 preadipocytes. Both 6,7,4 -THIF and daidzein were treated in the presence and absence of mixture of isobutylmethylxanthine, dexamethasone, and insulin (MDI). We observed that 6,7,4 -THIF, but not daidzein, inhibited MDI-induced adipogenesis significantly at 40 and 80 -M, associated with decreased peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor- and C/EBP- protein expression. 6,7,4 -THIF significantly sup pressed MDI-induced lipid accumulation in the early stage of adipogenesis, attributable to a suppression of cell proliferation and the induction of cell cycle arrest. We also determined that 6,7,4 -THIF, but not daidzein, attenuated phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase (PI3K)/Akt signaling pathway. 6,7,4 -THIF was found to inhibit PI3K activity via direct binding in an ATP-competitive manner.

Conclusion: Our results suggest that 6,7,4 -THIF suppresses adipogenesis in 3T3-L1 preadipocytes by directly targeting PI3K. Soy isoflavones like 6,7,4 -THIF may have potential for development into novel treatment strategies for chronic obesity.

## 1 Introduction

In recent decades, the prevalence of obesity has been rapidly increasing, posing significant social and economic challenges. Obesity occurs when the size and number of adipocytes become excessive, and increases the risk of other chronic diseases, including type 2 diabetes and cardiovascular diseases. Preadipocytes differentiate into mature adipocytes through a process called adipogenesis, involving mitotic clonal expansion (MCE) and terminal differentiation [1]. MCE is an essential prerequisite step preceding terminal differentiation and is therefore crucial to the entire adipogenic process [2, 3]. During MCE, cells in G0 phase synchronously re-enter the cell cycle, and cell numbers increase, a process termed as adipocyte hyperplasia. In addition, hormonal stimulation can increase the size of existing preadipocytes, leading to adipocyte hypertrophy. Therefore, the suppression of MCE during adipogenesis of 3T3-L1 preadipocytes represents a potential strategy for the prevention and treatment of obesity.

3T3-L1 preadipocytes are a commonly employed model system for studying the anti-adipogenic activity of specific compounds. 3T3-L1 preadipocytes become mature adipocytes under hormonal stimulation, which can be achieved with a mixture of dexamethasone, isobutylmethylxanthine (IBMX), and insulin [4]. IBMX and dexamethasone increase the transcriptional activity of CCAAT-enhancer-binding protein  (C/EBP-) and C/EBP-, respectively, further enhancing the expression of peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor- (PPAR-) and C/EBP- [5]. These two genes are major transcription factors expressed during the early stage of adipo genesis [6–10]. In addition, insulin activates PI3K/Akt- and ERK-mediated signaling pathways, which in turn initiate adipogenesis in 3T3-L1 preadipocytes [11] by regulating gene expression of PPAR- [12, 13].

As major isoflavones, daidzein and genistein are found in soybean at a concentration of 100–300 mg per 100 g of soybean [14]. However, only a small proportion (7–30%) of the ingested amount is likely to be detected in urine and less than 10% can be found in feces [15–17], suggesting that some degree of biotransformation occurs within the body. Daidzein is readily metabolized to produce equol in the human intestine [16], and is also hydroxylated by the action of the phase II enzyme cytochrome P450 (CYP1A2) [18] to produce 6,7,4 -trihydroxyisoflavone (6,7,4 -THIF) and 7,3 ,4 -trihydroxyisoflavone (7,3 ,4 -THIF) in the human liver [18–20]. Daidzein and its metabolites have been reported to exert potential beneficial effects on human health. For example, daidzein has been reported to exhibit lipolytic activity in 3T3- L1 cells [21] and anti-obesity effects in an animal model by activating thermogenesis [22]. Daidzein has also been reported to inhibit TNF--mediated adiponectin downregulation in 3T3-L1 cells [23]. Equol, an intestinal daidzein metabolite, has been demonstrated to reduce body and abdominal white adipose tissue masses in female rats [24]. Natural S-equol likely contributes to the prevention of cardiovascular diseases and diabetes by lowering LDL cholesterol, HbA1c, and CAVI scores, thereby controlling glycemia in overweight or obese populations [25]. Similarly, 6,7,4 -THIF, a bioactive metabolite produced in the liver, suppresses cyclin-dependent kinase 1 and 2 activity more potently than daidzein, leading to the inhibition of colon cancer cell proliferation [26]. However, the anti-adipogenic activities of 6,7,4 -THIF have not been previously investigated. In the present study, we investigated whether 6,7,4 -THIF more effectively inhibits the adipogenesis of 3T3-L1 preadipocytes than its parent compound, as well as the mechanisms by which this might occur.

## 2 Materials and methods

## 2.1 Chemicals

6,7,4 -THIF and daidzein were obtained from Indofine Chem ical Company (Hillsborough, NJ, USA). Antibodies against Akt and p-Akt, p70S6K and p-p70S6K, and GSK3- and p-GSK3- were purchased from Cell Signaling Biotechnology (Beverly, MA, USA). Dexamethasone, 3-isobutyl-1- methylxanthine (IBMX) and insulin were purchased from Sigma (St. Louis, MO, USA).

## 2.2 Cell culture and preadipocyte differentiation

All cell culture materials were obtained from GIBCO BRL (Grand Island, NY, USA). 3T3-L1 mouse preadipocytes were maintained in DMEM supplemented with 10% bovine calf serum (10% $\mathrm { C O } _ { 2 }$ and $3 7 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } )$ , and cultured until confluence. Confluent 3T3-L1 preadipocytes were incubated for 2 days in DMEM supplemented with 10% fetal bovine serum (FBS) and mixture of isobutylmethylxanthine, dexamethasone, and insulin (MDI) cocktail [dexamethasone (1 -M), IBMX (0.5 mM), and insulin (5 -g/mL)]. The medium was replaced every 2 days with fresh DMEM containing 10% FBS and 5 -g/mL of insulin.

## 2.3 Oil red O staining

3T3-L1 preadipocytes were seeded in 24-well plates at a den sity of $2 . 5 \times 1 0 ^ { 4 }$ cells per well and incubated until confluence. The cells were then incubated with DMEM supplemented with 10% FBS and MDI cocktail in the presence or absence of 6,7,4 -THIF and daidzein. The medium was then changed to DMEM supplemented with 10% FBS and 5 -g/mL of in sulin and replaced every 2 days After the induction of cell differentiation for 10 days. the media were removed, and the mature 3T3-L1 adipocytes were fixed in 10% formalin. The fixed cells were stained with Oil red O solution as previously described [27]. Stained Oil red O solution was eluted with isopropyl alcohol (Sigma), and the concentration was measured using a spectrophotometer set at 515 nm.

## 2.4 Multimodal coherent anti-stokes Raman scattering (CARS) microscope

CARS and two-photon excitation fluorescence (TPEF) imaging analysis were performed as described previously [28]. In brief, for CARS imaging of lipid droplets present in adipocytes, the wave-number difference between the pump laser and Stokes laser was tuned to $2 8 4 0 / \mathrm { c m }$ , which matches the Raman shift of the $\mathrm { C H } _ { 2 }$ symmetric stretch vibrations in lipid molecules. Combined beams were focused into the specimen through a 60 water immersion objective using a 1.2 numerical aperture. The forward CARS signal was collected with an air condenser (numerical aperture 0.55), transmitted through a 600/65-nm bandpass filter, and detected using a photomultiplier tube (PMT; H7422–40, Hamamatsu, Japan). For TPEF imaging of intracellular fluorescent 6,7,4 -THIF and daidzein, the backward signal was collected by the objective, spectrally separated from the excitation source, transmitted through a 520/40-nm bandpass filter and detected with a PMT (H7422–40, Hamamatsu) mounted on the back port of the microscope. Average acquisition time for a $5 1 2 \times 5 1 2$ pixel image was 1.12 s, and the combined Stokes and pump laser power directed at the specimen was maintained at a constant 55 mW. Images were analyzed with FluoView software (Olympus, Center Valley, PA, USA).

## 2.5 Trypan blue assay

3T3-L1 preadipocytes were seeded in 24-well plates at a density of $2 . 5 \times 1 0 ^ { 4 }$ cells per well, and incubated in DMEM supplemented with 10% FBS and MDI cocktail in the presence or absence of 6,7,4 -THIF or daidzein $( 8 0 ~ \mu \mathrm { M } )$ . Cells were trypsinized and stained with 0.4% Trypan blue. The stained cells were loaded onto a hematocytometer, and the viable cells were counted.

## 2.6 Flow cytometry using a fluorescence-activated cell sorter (FACS)

3T3-L1 preadipocytes were seeded in 6-cm dishes at a density of $1 . 5 \times 1 0 ^ { 5 }$ cells per dish, and incubated until confluence. Confluent cells were cultured with M DI cocktail and insulin $( 5 ~ \mu \mathrm { g } / \mathrm { m L } )$ with 6,7,4 -THIF or daidzein $( 8 0 ~ \mu \mathrm { M } )$ . After centrifugation (3 min, 1000 rpm), the pellets were resuspended in ice-cold 70% v/v ethanol and fixed overnight at $4 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } .$ . The cells were then centrifuged (3 min, 1500 rpm) and resuspended in 600 -L of PBS containing propidium iodide solution (20 -g/mL; Sigma) and RNase (0.2 mg/mL; Amresco; Solon, OH, USA). The mixture was incubated at $3 7 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ for 10 min in the dark. Fluorescence was measured using an FACS Calibur flow cytometer (Becton-Dickinson; San Jose, CA, USA). Ten thousand cells in each sample were analyzed.

## 2.7 Western blot assay

3T3-L1 preadipocytes were seeded in 6-cm dishes at a density of $1 . 5 \times 1 0 ^ { 5 }$ cells per dish and cultured for 2 days in 10% FBS, MDI cocktail, and insulin $( 5 ~ \mu \mathrm { g } / \mathrm { m L } )$ with $6 , 7 , 4 ^ { \prime } { \cdot } \mathrm { T H I F }$ or daidzein $( 2 0 , 4 0 , \mathrm { o r } 8 0 \mu \mathrm { M } )$ . The cells were lysed and centrifuged (10 min, 14 000 rpm, $4 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } ) .$ , and supernatants were collected. The supernatants were loaded onto 10% SDS-PAGE gels and transferred to nitrocellulose membranes (GE Healthcare; Piscataway, NJ, USA). The membranes were blocked with 5% skim milk and incubated with specific primary antibodies followed by HRP-conjugated secondary antibodies. Protein bands were visualized using a chemiluminescence detection kit (Amersham Pharmacia Biotech; Piscataway, NJ, USA).

## 2.8 PI3K kinase assay

Active PI3K protein (100 ng) was incubated with 6,7,4 -THIF or daidzein for 10 min at $3 0 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } .$ . Phosphatidylinositol (0.5 mg/mL; Avanti Polar Lipids; Alabaster, AL, USA) was added and incubated for 5 min at room temperature. Next, reaction buffer [100 mM HEPES (pH 7.6), 50 mM MgCl , and 250 -M ATP) containing 10 -Ci of $[ \gamma ^ { \ l _ { 3 } 2 } \mathrm { P } ]$ ATP was added and incubated for 10 min at $3 0 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } .$ The reaction was halted by adding 15 -L of 4N HCl and 130 $\mu \mathrm { L }$ of a chloroform and methanol mixture (1:1). After vortexing, 30 -L of the lower chloroform phase was spotted onto preactivated 1% potassium oxalate-coated, silica, gel plates. The spotted chlo roform phase was separated via thin-layer chromatography, and radio-labeled spots were visualized by autoradiography.

## 2.9 Pull-down assay

Sepharose 4B freeze-dried powder (0.3 g; GE Healthcare) was activated in HCl (1 mM) and suspended in $6 , 7 , 4 ^ { \prime } { \cdot } \mathrm { T H I F }$ coupled (2 mg) solution [0.1 M ${ \mathrm { N a H C O } } _ { 3 }$ and 0.5 M NaCl]. The mixture was rotated overnight at $4 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ before transfer to Tris-HCl buffer (0.1 M; pH 8.0), and rotated overnight at $4 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ . The following day, the mixture was washed in acetate buffer (0.1 M; pH 4.0) and Tris-HCl (0.1 M) NaCl buffer (0.5 M; pH 8.0), and suspended in PBS.

The pull-down assay was performed as previously de scribed [29]. In brief, active PI3K protein was incubated with Sepharose 4B alone, 6,7,4 -THIF-Sepharose 4B beads, or daidzein-Sepharose 4B in reaction buffer. After incubation at $4 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } ,$ , the beads were washed in washing buffer, and proteins bound to the beads were analyzed via Western blot assay.

## 2.10 Molecular modeling

Insight II (Accelrys, San Diego, CA, USA) was used for the docking study and structure analysis with the crystal coordinates of PI3K in complex with ATP (accession code 1E8X) available at the Protein Data Bank (http://www.rcsb.org/pdb/).

## 2.11 Statistical analysis

Data are expressed as means SD, and Student’s t-test was used for single statistical comparisons. A probability value of p < 0.05 was used as the criterion for statistical significance.

## 3 Results and discussion

## 3.1 6,7,4- -THIF inhibits MDI-induced adipogenesis in 3T3-L1 preadipocytes via downregulation of peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor-- (PPAR--) and C/EBP- protein expression

6,7,4 -THIF is structurally identical to daidzen, except for an additional hydroxyl group on ring A at the 6 position (Fig. 1A and B). We attempted to assess the effect of the additional hydroxyl group on anti-adipogenic activity in 3T3-L1 preadipocytes. Cytotoxic effect of 6,7,4 -THIF was determined (Supporting Information Fig. S1), and cells were treated with 6,7,4 -THIF or daidzein followed by Oil red O solution. MDI treatment was observed to induce a greater accumulation of lipid droplets when compared to controls without MDI treatment. In addition, 6,7,4 -THIF treatment (20 to 80 -M) more effectively attenuated MDI-induced lipid accumulation than daidzein treatment (Fig. 2A). In support of this result, quantitative analysis revealed that MDI induces threefold greater lipid accumulation compared to controls (p < 0.01). While lipid contents were unaffected by the treatment of daidzein at 20, 40, or 80 -M, 6,7,4 -THIF at 40 and 80 -M signifi cantly reduced MDI-induced lipid accumulation by up to 67% (Fig. 2B; $p < 0 . 0 1 )$ . Importantly, the extent of lipid contents in cells treated with 80 -M 6,7,4 -THIF was similar to the controls (Fig. 2B). 6,7,4 -THIF was most effective in reduc ing lipid contents of 3T3-L1 preadipocytes among various daidzein metabolites, including 7,3 ,4 -THIF, 7,8,4 -THIF, and equol (Supporting Information Fig. S2).

The anti-adipogenic properties of 6,7,4 -THIF in 3T3- L1 preadipocytes were further confirmed by CARS/TPEF imaging analysis. Consistent with the Oil red O staining data, 6,7,4 -THIF markedly reduced lipid accumulation triggered by MDI treatment, whereas the same concentrations of daidzein were not observed to have similar effects (Fig. 2C). We further attempted to ascertain whether 6,7,4 -THIF or daidzein, both of which are hydrophobic, exert effects within cells after penetrating the plasma membrane. Since 6,7,4 - THIF and daidzein are naturally fluorescent molecules, their green fluorescence inside cells could be detected (Fig. 2C). Green fluorescence indicative of 6,7,4 -THIF and daidzein was observed within cells (cytoplasm) after differentiation. Therefore, the possibility exists that the anti-adipogenic effects of both 6,7,4 -THIF and daidzein may be due to their influence on adipogenic signaling cascades within the cytoplasm.

We next evaluated whether the decreases in intracellular lipid contents were associated with lower levels of PPAR- and C/EBP-, two major adipogenic proteins expressed in the early stage of adipogenesis [30, 31]. 6,7,4 -THIF (80 -M) markedly suppressed MDI-induced upregulation of PPAR- and C/EBP-, which was increased by the same concentra tion of daidzein after 8 days of MDI treatment (Fig. 2D). Expression of both adipogenic marker proteins was not detected after 2 days of MDI treatment, representing the early stage of adipogenesis (Fig. 2D). Taken together, our results suggest that 6,7,4 -THIF is more effective than daidzen for the attenuation of MDI-induced adipogenesis in 3T3-L1 preadipocytes via downregulation of PPAR- and C/EBP- expression.

## 3.2 6,7,4- -THIF suppresses early stage of adipogenesis

The process of adipogenesis in 3T3-L1 preadipocytes consists of an early stage (MCE; Days 0–2), a postmitotic intermediate stage (Days 2–4) and a terminal stage (after Day 4) [32]. To identify the timepoint most affected by 6,7,4 -THIF during differentiation, 6,7,4 -THIF was treated at each of the differ ent stages [27]. Oil red O staining revealed that 6,7,4 -THIF reduces lipid accumulation when treated during Day 0–2 (Fig. 3A and B). In fact, treatment of 6,7,4 -THIF at Days 0–2, 0–4, and 0–6 significantly decreased MDI-triggered lipid accumulation by more than 50%, similar to the levels observed in controls without MDI treatment. This suggests that the treatment of $6 , 7 , 4 ^ { \prime } { \cdot } \mathrm { T H I F }$ at the early stage (MCE) effectively suppresses adipogenesis of 3T3-L1 preadipocytes regardless of the total number of treatment days.

## 3.3 6,7,4- -THIF inhibits MDI-induced cell proliferation and subsequently delays cell cycle progression

We sought to investigate whether 6,7,4 -THIF-mediated suppression of MCE could be attributable to suppression of cell proliferation and/or cell cycle arrest. Changes in total cel numbers over 24 and 48 h were evaluated using a Trypan blue assay. As expected, MDI treatment caused an increase in cell number up to 48 h, while $6 , 7 , 4 ^ { \prime } { \cdot } \mathrm { T H I F }$ (but not daidzein), significantly suppressed this increase $( p < 0 . 0 5 ; \mathrm { F i g . } 4 \mathrm { A } )$ . We then evaluated whether the 6,7,4 -THIF-mediated decrease in cell number was associated with cell cycle arrest during MCE. Cell cycle progression was measured using FACS analysis and the population of cells in each cell cycle phase was quantified. Control cells were predominantly within G1 phase until 48 h (Fig. 4B and C). MDI treatment stimulated cell cycle progression, evidenced by a greater proportion of total cells ( 30%) entering S phase at 16-h treatment, which continuously progressed into G2/M phase by 24 h of MDI stimulation. MDI-treated cells re-entered G1 phase within the next cell cycle at 48 h. Similarly, 30 and 20% of cells treated with daidzein (80 -M) entered S phase at 16 and 24 h of treatment, respectively (Fig. 4B and C). The cells entered G2/M phase, and exited M phase at 24 h of treatment, before re-entering G1 phase within the next cell cycle at 48 h. Interestingly, the majority of the cells (87.5% of the total) was arrested in G1 phase after 16 h of treatment with 6,7,4 -THIF and 70% of total cells were arrested at the G1 phase by 48 h (Fig. 4B and C). Collectively, these results indicate that $^ { 6 , 7 , 4 ^ { \prime } - }$ THIF, but not daidzein, suppresses MDI-induced cell proliferation of 3T3-L1 preadipocytes by retarding cell cycle progression, which together contributes to suppress MCE during adipogenesis.

## 3.4 6,7,4- -THIF, but not daidzein, suppresses MDI-induced Akt/p70S6K/GSK3- phosphorylation by inhibiting PI3K activity

The MEK/ERK and PI3K/Akt signaling cascades play an important role in regulating insulin-mediated cell proliferation [33]. Interestingly, we observed that $6 , 7 , 4 ^ { \prime } { \cdot } \mathrm { T H I F }$ treatment reduced MEK/ERK expression to a lesser extent than the PI3K/Akt-mediated signaling pathway (data not shown). Based on this observation, we hypothesized that 6,7,4 -THIF may exert its anti-adipogenic effects in MCE via inhibition of the PI3K/Akt-mediated signaling pathway. Thus, we attempted to further evaluate the regulatory effects of $^ { 6 , 7 , 4 ^ { \prime } . }$ THIF and daidzein on Akt and its downstream signaling proteins, including $\mathrm { p } 7 0 ^ { \mathrm { S 6 K } }$ and GSK3-, which are known regulators of the cell cycle [34]. Western blot analysis revealed that MDI-induced phosphorylation of Akt, $\mathrm { p } 7 0 ^ { \mathrm { s 6 \dot { K } } }$ , and GSK3-  were unchanged by daidzein treatment (20, 40, and 80 -M) (Fig. 5A). However, treatment of $6 , 7 , 4 ^ { \prime } { \cdot } \mathrm { T H I F } ,$ , especially at 80 -M, markedly decreased protein expression levels of phospho-Akt, $\mathsf { p } 7 0 ^ { \mathsf { S 6 K } }$ , and GSK3- (Fig. 5A).

6,7,4 -THIF (20 to 80 -M), but not daidzein, was found to markedly suppress the activity of PI3K, an upstream regulator of Akt (Fig. 5B). 6,7,4 -THIF (20 -M) suppressed PI3K activity levels observed in cells treated with 20 -M of the common PI3K inhibitor LY294002. Higher levels of $6 , 7 , 4 ^ { \prime } { \cdot } \mathrm { T H I F }$ (40 and 80 $\mu \mathrm { M } )$ suppressed PI3K activity to a greater extent than 20 -M LY294002 (Fig. 5B). Taken together, these results indicate that 6,7,4 -THIF, but not daidzein, blocks MDIinduced activation of the Akt-mediated signaling pathway by inhibiting PI3K activity, thereby contributing to the suppression of MDI-induced adipogenesis.

## 3.5 6,7,4- -THIF directly binds to PI3K in an ATP competitive manner

To evaluate the nature of the interaction between 6.7.4'-THIF and PI3K, a pull-down assay was performed. We observed that PI3K was precipitated with 6,7,4 -THIF-conjugated Sepharose 4B beads (Fig. 6A, lane 4) but not with unconjugated or daidzein-conjugated Sepharose-4B beads (Fig. 6A, lane 2 and 3). This indicates that 6,7,4 -THIF directly binds to PI3K, a likely method by which it inhibits its activity and affects subsequent activation of downstream signaling cascades. Moreover, binding between 6,7,4 -THIF and PI3K decreased as the concentration of ATP increased (Fig. 6B), indicating that 6,7,4 -THIF inhibits PI3K activity in an ATP-competitive manner.

We next used a modeling study using the known crystal structure of PI3K in complex with ATP to gain further insights into the PI3K-6,7,4 -THIF complex [36]. PI3K consists of four domains: a Ras-binding domain, a C2 domain, a helical domain, and a catalytic domain. The catalytic domain of the enzyme consists of an N-lobe and a C-lobe with a fold similar to protein kinases, and this structural similarity is also conserved in the ATP-binding site, which is flanked by these two lobes [37]. Consequently, ATP binds between these lobe in a manner similar to ATP binding with protein kinases. The N- and C-lobes are linked through a loop, which is called the “hinge region”. The backbone of this loop interacts with the adenine moiety of ATP via hydrogen bonding. It was found to be plausible that the hydroxyl groups at positions 6 and 7 of $6 , 7 , 4 ^ { \prime } { \cdot } \mathrm { T H I F }$ could form hydrogen bonds with the backbone atoms of Val882 in the hinge region of PI3K.

The hydroxyl group at the 4 position might also form hydrogen bonds with the side chains of Asp836. Additionally, 6,7,4 -THIF could be sandwiched by the side chains of the hydrophobic residues in the ATP-binding site, including Met804, Trp812, Ile831, Leu838, Tyr867, Ile879 from the Nlobe and Ala885, Met953, Phe961, Ile963 from the C-lobe. The potent inhibitory activity of 6,7,4 -THIF toward PI3K might be attributable to these hydrogen bonds and hydrophobic interactions. We additionally found physical evidence that 6,7,4 - THIF directly binds with PI3K (Fig. 6C). Collectively, our results suggest that 6,7,4 -THIF binds to PI3K in the cytosol, where it inhibits the activity of PI3K and downstream signaling cascades, leading to the suppression of adipogenesis in 3T3-L1 preadipocytes.

## 4 Concluding remarks

Our results suggest that 6,7,4 -THIF suppresses adipogenesis in 3T3-L1 preadipocytes, especially at the early stage, via direct binding and inhibition of PI3K activity. While the anti-adipogenic activity of genistein has been frequently stud ied [38–41], relatively few similar studies have been conducted for daidzein [21–23]. This could be due to the fact that daidzen is more readily metabolized than genistein, and therefore clinical benefits less likely to eventuate [42]. Our study is the first to compare the anti-adipogenic activity of daidzen with one of its metabolites. Oil red O staining showed that, compared to daidzein and other daidzein metabolites (equol, 7,3 ,4 -THIF and 7,8,4 -THIF), 6,7,4 -THIF is more effective in reducing lipid contents of 3T3-L1 preadipocytes (Supporting Information Fig. S2). This suggests that the additional hydroxyl group located in ring A (C-6) contributes to enhanced anti-adipogenic activity of 6,7,4 -THIF.

Based on the previous studies that have shown a correlation between dietary isoflavone intake and plasma isoflavone concentration [43], 2.2 g of isoflavone intake can be expected to result in an approximate 80 -M plasma concentration. However, there have been no studies into the quantity of 6,7,4 - THIF produced from daidzein consumption. Depending on the conversion rate of 6,7,4 -THIF, the required consumption of isoflavone could be expected to vary from as low as a couple of grams to high as 20 grams. The long-term consumption of isoflavone could cause sustained and prolonged effects of 6,7,4 -THIF in the body, enabling lower concentrations to exert anti-obesity effects. Furthermore, dietary supplements with high concentrations of daidzein could help to achieve the concentrations required for beneficial effects of 6,7,4 -THIF. Further studies are required to determine the mechanisms by which physiological levels of 6,7,4 -THIF and daidzein regulate obesity in humans and animal models.
